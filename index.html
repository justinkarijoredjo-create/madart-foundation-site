MADART FOUNDATION · Amsterdam

We curate the blind spots
of the art world.

The median Dutch visual artist earns €16,000 a year — the lowest of any creative profession, well below the social minimum. Funds finance what institutions already show. Galleries show what is already sold. MADART Foundation exists for who is left outside that circle.

What We Do

MADART Foundation curates contemporary makers overlooked by the traditional art world — self-taught, uncategorisable, or simply outside institutional criteria — and gives them visibility on landmark locations in Amsterdam.

We finance their exhibitions: production, presentation, fee. 100% of sales proceeds go to the maker. No exclusive contract. No fee to participate.

Who We Curate

The system didn't overlook these makers because their work falls short.
It overlooked them because the system is too narrow to contain them.

The Autodidact

No recognised diploma, so no official start date for funds that require institutional credentials.

The Uncategorisable

Working between disciplines. The work falls just outside every recognisable box.

The Too-Versatile Maker

A practice too broad for the institutional expectation of one consistent signature.

The Experienced Outsider

Decades of work, never institutional recognition. Quality has no expiry date.

How We Curate

The Dimensions

Every maker, every work, every exhibition is tested against four conditions. Each is necessary. A maker who meets three of the four is not curated.

Future-Oriented

Points forward in content, aesthetics or material. Not nostalgic — direction-giving.

Joyful

Activates a positive, connecting response in the viewer.

Optimistic

Forward-looking and human-centred. Critical is welcome. Cynical is not.

Innovative

Objectively new in form, medium, technique or material.

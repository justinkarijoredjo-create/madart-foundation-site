MADART FOUNDATION

We curate the blind spots
of the art world.

The median Dutch visual artist earns €16,000 a year — the lowest of any creative profession, below the social minimum. Funds finance what institutions already show. Galleries show what is already sold. MADART Foundation exists for who is left outside that circle.

What MADART Foundation Does

MADART Foundation curates contemporary makers overlooked by the traditional art world — self-taught, uncategorisable, or simply outside institutional criteria — and gives them visibility on landmark locations in Amsterdam. 100% of sales proceeds go to the maker.

Who We Curate

The Autodidact

No recognised diploma, so no official start date for funds that require institutional credentials.

The Uncategorisable

Working between disciplines. The work falls just outside every recognisable box.

The Too-Versatile Maker

A practice too broad for the institutional expectation of one consistent signature.

The Experienced Outsider

Decades of work, never institutional recognition. Quality has no expiry date.

How We Curate — The Dimensions

Future-Oriented

Points forward in content, aesthetics or material.

Joyful

Activates a positive, connecting response.

Optimistic

Forward-looking and human-centred. Critical is welcome, cynical is not.

Innovative

Objectively new in form, medium or technique.
